# AIR POLLUTION

Air Prevention and Control of Pollution Act 1981 prohibits any person or industry from emitting air pollutants beyond prescribed standards without obtaining consent from the State Pollution Control Board.

Air Prevention and Control of Pollution Act 1981 Section 22 makes it an offence for any industrial plant to emit smoke, dust, fumes, or toxic gases beyond permissible limits set by the Pollution Control Board.

Environment Protection Act 1986 Rule 3 empowers the Central Government to set standards for emission of environmental pollutants from industries, operations, or processes.

Environment Protection Act 1986 Section 7 prohibits any person from emitting or discharging environmental pollutants in excess of prescribed standards.

National Ambient Air Quality Standards NAAQS 2009 set by CPCB define permissible concentration limits for pollutants like PM2.5, PM10, SO2, NO2, and CO in ambient air.

Motor Vehicles Act 1988 Section 190 penalizes use of vehicles emitting smoke or exhaust gases beyond permissible pollution control norms set by the government.

Industries Development and Regulation Act mandates that all industries obtain No Objection Certificate from State Pollution Control Board before commencing operations that may cause air pollution.

Manufacture Storage and Import of Hazardous Chemicals Rules 1989 regulate industries handling toxic chemicals that can cause air pollution and mandate emergency response plans.

Energy Conservation Act 2001 promotes use of cleaner fuels and energy-efficient technologies in industries to reduce air pollution and carbon emissions.

National Clean Air Programme NCAP 2019 targets 20 to 30 percent reduction in PM2.5 and PM10 concentrations by 2024 in 122 non-attainment cities across India.

Graded Response Action Plan GRAP mandates emergency measures in Delhi NCR and surrounding areas when air quality deteriorates to severe or emergency levels.

Municipal Corporation bylaws prohibit open burning of garbage, dry leaves, and construction waste within municipal limits as it causes severe air pollution.

# WATER POLLUTION

Water Prevention and Control of Pollution Act 1974 Section 24 prohibits any person or industry from knowingly causing or permitting poisonous, noxious, or polluting matter to enter any stream, well, sewer, or land.

Water Prevention and Control of Pollution Act 1974 Section 25 mandates that any industry, operation, or process likely to discharge sewage or trade effluent must obtain prior consent from the State Pollution Control Board.

Environment Protection Act 1986 Schedule I sets effluent discharge standards for industries including textile, tannery, distillery, paper, and chemical industries discharging into water bodies.

Environment Protection Rules 1986 prohibit discharge of untreated industrial effluents into rivers, lakes, groundwater, or coastal waters beyond prescribed concentration limits.

National Green Tribunal Act 2010 empowers the NGT to take suo motu cognizance of water pollution cases and impose penalties on polluters including industries and municipalities.

Ganga Action Plan and National River Conservation Plan mandate that no untreated sewage or industrial effluent be discharged into the Ganga river or its tributaries.

Coastal Regulation Zone Notification 2011 prohibits discharge of untreated effluents and solid waste into coastal waters, estuaries, and mangrove areas.

Bureau of Indian Standards IS 10500 sets permissible limits for drinking water quality including pH, turbidity, dissolved solids, and microbial contamination.

Hazardous Waste Management Rules 2016 prohibit disposal of hazardous chemical waste into water bodies, open land, or municipal drains without proper treatment.

Underground Water Extraction Regulations mandate permission from Central Ground Water Authority before extraction of groundwater in notified areas to prevent depletion and contamination.

Factories Act 1948 Section 13 mandates that every factory ensure proper disposal of trade effluents and prohibits discharge of untreated waste into public drains or water bodies.

Swachh Bharat Mission guidelines mandate that all urban local bodies ensure 100 percent collection and treatment of sewage before disposal into water bodies.

# NOISE POLLUTION

Noise Pollution Regulation and Control Rules 2000 under Environment Protection Act prescribe ambient noise standards for industrial, commercial, residential, and silence zones during day and night hours.

Noise Pollution Regulation and Control Rules 2000 Rule 5 prohibits use of loudspeakers, public address systems, and sound producing instruments in silence zones and during nighttime hours between 10 PM and 6 AM.

Environment Protection Act 1986 Section 6 empowers the government to restrict noise levels from industries, construction sites, generators, and public gatherings to protect public health.

Factories Act 1948 mandates that all factory premises take adequate measures to protect workers from excessive industrial noise and maintain noise levels within prescribed occupational limits.

Motor Vehicles Act 1988 prohibits use of multitone horns, air horns, and pressure horns on vehicles in residential areas and near hospitals and schools.

The Code on Wages and Occupational Safety Health and Working Conditions Code 2020 mandates noise control measures in industrial establishments to protect worker health from occupational noise exposure.

Bureau of Indian Standards IS 4954 provides specifications for noise barriers and sound insulation requirements for industrial and commercial buildings near residential areas.

Municipal Corporation bylaws in most states prohibit operation of diesel generator sets, industrial machinery, and construction equipment beyond permissible noise limits during nighttime hours.

Supreme Court Order 2005 in Noise Pollution case restricts use of firecrackers and loudspeakers during nighttime and mandates strict enforcement of noise pollution rules by state governments.

# SOLID WASTE

Solid Waste Management Rules 2016 mandate that every household, commercial establishment, and institution segregate waste at source into wet waste, dry waste, and hazardous waste categories before disposal.

Solid Waste Management Rules 2016 Rule 4 prohibit littering of solid waste in public places, open spaces, water bodies, drains, and roads and mandate door to door collection by urban local bodies.

Solid Waste Management Rules 2016 Rule 16 prohibit burning of solid waste in open areas and mandate scientific processing and disposal of all municipal solid waste.

Environment Protection Act 1986 empowers authorities to take strict penal action against industries, municipalities, or individuals illegally dumping solid waste causing environmental pollution.

Plastic Waste Management Rules 2016 prohibit manufacture, sale, and use of single use plastics below 75 microns thickness and mandate Extended Producer Responsibility for plastic packaging waste.

Plastic Waste Management Amendment Rules 2021 ban identified single use plastic items from July 2022 and mandate plastic waste collection and recycling by producers and importers.

Hazardous Waste Management Rules 2016 mandate that industries generating hazardous solid waste obtain authorization from State Pollution Control Board and dispose waste only in approved facilities.

Biomedical Waste Management Rules 2016 mandate segregation, treatment, and disposal of biomedical waste from hospitals, clinics, and laboratories in color coded bags and containers.

E-Waste Management Rules 2016 mandate that producers, manufacturers, and consumers of electronic goods ensure proper collection and channelization of e-waste to authorized dismantlers and recyclers.

Construction and Demolition Waste Management Rules 2016 mandate that generators of construction debris ensure storage, collection, and disposal of waste only at designated sites approved by local authorities.

Swachh Bharat Mission Urban guidelines mandate that all urban local bodies achieve 100 percent door to door waste collection, source segregation, and scientific processing of municipal solid waste.

National Green Tribunal directions mandate that no municipality or industry create open dumpsites or landfills without proper leachate collection, gas extraction, and groundwater protection measures.

# WATERLOGGING AND FLOODING

Disaster Management Act 2005 Section 31 mandates that District Disaster Management Authority prepare district disaster management plans including flood mitigation, early warning, and evacuation procedures.

Disaster Management Act 2005 Section 34 empowers District Disaster Management Authority to take all measures for prevention of waterlogging, urban flooding, and related disasters.

Model Building Bylaws 2016 mandate that all new constructions provide adequate stormwater drainage, rainwater harvesting structures, and permeable surfaces to prevent waterlogging.

National Disaster Management Guidelines on Urban Flooding 2010 mandate that all municipal corporations prepare urban flood management plans, maintain stormwater drains, and prevent encroachment on natural drainage channels.

Environment Protection Act 1986 prohibits encroachment on natural drains, wetlands, and floodplains that disrupts natural water flow and causes urban flooding and waterlogging.

Wetlands Conservation and Management Rules 2017 prohibit encroachment, filling, and construction on wetlands, ponds, and natural water bodies that serve as flood buffers in urban and rural areas.

Municipal Solid Waste Management Rules prohibit disposal of solid waste into stormwater drains and natural channels as it causes blockages leading to urban waterlogging during rainfall.

National Water Policy 2012 mandates that state governments prepare comprehensive flood management plans including maintenance of drainage networks, flood embankments, and early warning systems.

Urban Local Body Acts of various states mandate that municipal corporations maintain stormwater drainage networks, desilting of drains before monsoon, and removal of encroachments on natural drainage paths.

Central Water Commission guidelines mandate regular inspection and maintenance of irrigation canals, embankments, and drainage channels to prevent breaches and waterlogging in agricultural and urban areas.

# ROAD POTHOLES

National Highways Act 1956 Section 8A mandates that the National Highways Authority of India maintain national highways in good repair and ensure safety of road users.

Motor Vehicles Act 1988 Section 138 empowers state governments to make rules for maintenance of roads and removal of hazards including potholes, damaged surfaces, and broken road infrastructure.

Public Works Department Manual mandates that state PWD officials conduct regular inspection of state roads, repair potholes within prescribed timelines, and maintain roads in motorable condition.

National Highway Authority of India Concession Agreements mandate that highway concessionaires maintain road surface condition, repair potholes within 24 to 48 hours of reporting, and ensure user safety.

Indian Roads Congress IRC 37 guidelines specify standards for road pavement design, maintenance schedules, and distress repair procedures including pothole patching and resurfacing.

Consumer Protection Act 2019 allows citizens to file complaints against road authorities for deficiency in service when poorly maintained roads cause accidents, vehicle damage, or loss of life.

Right to Safe Roads as recognized by various High Courts mandates that road owning agencies ensure safe road conditions and compensate victims of accidents caused by potholes and poor road maintenance.

National Road Safety Policy 2010 mandates that all road owning agencies including NHAI, PWD, and municipal bodies prioritize road maintenance to eliminate road hazards and reduce accident fatalities.

Municipal Corporation Acts of various states mandate that urban local bodies maintain internal city roads, repair potholes within stipulated timelines, and ensure safe road conditions for citizens.

# ILLEGAL CONSTRUCTION

Urban Land Ceiling and Regulation Act prohibits unauthorized construction, illegal land use change, and encroachment on public land without prior approval from competent urban planning authorities.

Model Building Bylaws 2016 mandate that no person shall construct, reconstruct, or alter any building without obtaining prior building permission from the local authority or urban development authority.

Environmental Impact Assessment Notification 2006 mandates that large construction projects obtain prior environmental clearance from Ministry of Environment Forest and Climate Change before commencement.

Town and Country Planning Acts of various states empower planning authorities to demolish unauthorized constructions, seal illegal buildings, and penalize owners of illegal constructions.

Prevention of Encroachment Act empowers municipal authorities to remove illegal encroachments on public land, footpaths, roads, parks, and government property without prior notice in emergency situations.

Real Estate Regulation and Development Act RERA 2016 mandates that all real estate projects above prescribed size obtain registration and prohibits construction without approved plans and necessary clearances.

Forest Conservation Act 1980 prohibits any construction activity in forest land without prior approval of the Central Government and State Forest Department.

Coastal Regulation Zone Notification 2011 prohibits construction within prescribed distances from high tide line in coastal areas and mandates demolition of unauthorized structures in CRZ areas.

Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act 1958 prohibits any construction within 100 meters of protected monuments and regulates construction between 100 and 300 meters.

Fire Services Act mandates that all commercial and high rise buildings obtain No Objection Certificate from Fire Department confirming compliance with fire safety norms before construction approval.

# TREE CUTTING AND DEFORESTATION

Forest Conservation Act 1980 Section 2 prohibits any state government or authority from diverting forest land for non-forest purposes without prior approval of the Central Government.

Forest Conservation Act 1980 mandates that whenever forest land is diverted for development projects, compensatory afforestation must be carried out on equivalent non-forest degraded land.

Indian Forest Act 1927 prohibits felling, girdling, lopping, tapping, or burning of trees in reserved forests and protected forests without written permission from the Forest Department.

Environment Protection Act 1986 empowers the Central Government to restrict tree felling activities that cause environmental degradation and direct restoration of degraded forest ecosystems.

Biological Diversity Act 2002 mandates protection of biodiversity rich areas, heritage trees, and ecologically sensitive zones from destruction, felling, or encroachment.

Tree Preservation Orders under Municipal Corporation Acts of various states prohibit felling or pruning of trees above prescribed girth without obtaining prior permission from the Tree Authority.

National Forest Policy 1988 mandates maintenance of minimum 33 percent geographical area under forest and tree cover and strict restriction on felling of trees in ecologically sensitive areas.

Wildlife Protection Act 1972 prohibits destruction of habitat including trees inside wildlife sanctuaries, national parks, and tiger reserves and mandates strict action against encroachment.

Scheduled Tribes and Other Traditional Forest Dwellers Recognition of Forest Rights Act 2006 protects community forest rights and prohibits destruction of forests without consent of Gram Sabha.

State Tree Felling Regulation Acts in Punjab, Maharashtra, Karnataka, and other states mandate that citizens and agencies obtain permission from designated Tree Officer before felling any tree on public or private land.

Compensatory Afforestation Fund Act 2016 mandates collection of funds from project proponents diverting forest land and utilization of funds exclusively for afforestation and forest conservation activities.

National Green Tribunal has directed all state governments to ensure that no trees are felled for infrastructure projects without prior permission and adequate compensatory plantation of three trees for every tree felled.
